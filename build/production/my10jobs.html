Van Quoc Bui

My name is Van Quoc Bui,
and this is the story of how I pursued my passion.

At the age of six, I was diagnosed with “Aphasia”, meaning I had difficulties with speaking, listening, reading and writing. Despite having this disability, I always try to aim high and live life to the fullest.

I have been interested in creative artwork, as long as I can remember. However, my struggles with language and artwork prevented me from studying Graphic Design. After two years of constant rejections, I decided to gain life experience by hard work and intuition. School became more like an option than a necessity. Throughout the years, I had worked for many companies in various industries. Even though I gained a lot of experience and knowledge, it made me realise even more that my ambition lies in technology and design.

Eventually, in 2016, I committed myself to become a web developer. Without any educational background, I was dedicated to self-study and hard work. Within four months, I was given the chance to start in the desired industry. Now, I am able to enjoy doing creative work with great passion. This opportunity has taught me that there is more in life than we think. Life experience cannot be learned from books. You have to be open for it, go out and challenge yourself to gain experience.

Now that I have found myself, I want to do something back for the community. I have challenged myself to travel to Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam and work at ten low paid jobs in ten days. I want to learn from the people and help them chase their passion.

This is a non-profit initiative and an experiment, I want to share with the world. I hope inspire people to empathize with others and realise that everyone can make a difference with a good deed. Everyone has a passion, but not all of them has the opportunity to chase it. Some might even have given up on it. Hopefully, I will be able to help these individuals to find their passion or even chase it.

My approach About

What is your passion?

On the 4th of June, I will be traveling to Ho Chi Minh City in Vietnam. I will be working at ten low paid jobs in ten days to place myself in someone else’s shoes. I want to understand their feelings and hardships and help them find their passion.

I want to share the stories of ten individuals, who have endured hardships caused by poverty, health, disabilities or other disadvantages. By sharing these stories, I hope to inspire people to empathize with others and realise that everyone can make a difference with a good deed.

I will spend a day working with every individual and learn more about their life conditions. I hope to be able to give them something valuable back by helping them find or even pursue their passion. It could be as simple as providing books or other tools to payments for medicines or examinations.

    Day 1

    Recyclable materials seller

    The advice of the hotel employee

    Based on the advice of the hotel employee, I went to district 10. The hotel employee worked as a financial advisor in the daytime and knew in which neighbourhoods I could look for the volunteer jobs.

    After my arrival at district 10, I observed the surroundings and asked people if I could help them with their work. Unfortunately, I got turned down because of my healthy appearance.

    After several attempts, one lady agreed to let me help her and I called her Co Mai. She let me pick recyclable objects from the streets and push her cart. Later on, I helped separating the collection of recyclable items, which Co Mai had bought during our walk.

    About co Mai

    Co Mai comes from the countryside and started her family at a young age. When she was around 25 years old, she already had two children. Growing up on the countryside, Co Mai only knew how to farm and grow rice. That was how she earned her money and she didn’t dream of doing anything else, because she didn’t know anything else. When (country) people went to the cities to do business, they would bring others along. That was how Co Mai came to the city. She was brought to the city, but had to figure everything out by herself.

    We are country people and now we are among the people from the city. If we don’t have the knowledge or certificates, it is really hard to find a job.

    Now she has a job, where she can be her own boss. She buys or picks recycle objects and sells it to others. She likes this job, because she has the freedom to do anything. The money she earns is enough for the rent of their home. Her husband is working as well. His earnings are meant for food and sending their children to schools among others. She tells her children "We are country people and now we are among the people from the city. If we don’t have the knowledge or certificates, it is really hard to find a job".

    It’s my hometown, I can’t leave it

    Co Mai has already decided to go back to the country side, once her children are graduated and ready for a job. Currently, she goes back to the country side for special occasions, such as commemorations, marriages and Lunar’s New Year.

    Own experience

    I had really underestimated the job. Even when the cart was empty at the beginning, I was already sweating after pushing it for five minutes. I think that the environment made it harder for me, because the sun was shining very bright and the air was dirty.

    I was happy Co Mai let me work with her. It gave me hope that I could do volunteer jobs despite my appearances or the language barrier. She welcomed me with open arms and cared for me. It was fun to notice that she enjoyed the experience as well. She told my story to her sister and friends. Everyone welcomed me and didn’t treat me as an outsider. I have a lot of appreciation for Co Mai, because she is doing this tough job to support her family and give her children a better life.

    Gift for Co Mai

    It was hard to find Co Mai for the donation, because she has no permanent place where she does her job.

    Eventually, I was able to find her and give her the donations. During the interview, she didn’t show that she had a passion or dream. So based on her activities, I decided to give her a toolkit, which she could use to separate the collections of recyclable objects. This activity was hard to do with bare hands and that was why I thought it could come in handy. The rest of the money was given to her, so she could have a day off, spend it on her children or buy medicine among others.

    Day 2

    Carpet seller

    The language barrier

    To search for the second job, I went to district 3. After searching for a while, I found someone who was selling carpets. His name was Viet.

    He was a little sceptic and suspicious at first, so I told him my reasons. I showed him pictures from the first day as well and was able to calm him.

    He let me work with him and interact with the customers. I had to approach customers, ask what they were looking for and give them the carpet. The payment went through Viet first, but eventually he let me hold on some cash, so I could do the payments too.

    Even though the communication between Viet and me went difficult due to the language barrier, I had fun working with him. However, it was hard to understand each other during the interview, so only little can be written about Viet.

    About Viet

    Viet is a 25 year old boy, who makes his earnings with selling carpets on the streets. Within five years, Viet hopes to having a own farmer of vegetables. Working 15 hours a day, he is trying to save up some money to fulfil his dream. In addition, he is also helping his brother financially, whose restaurant went bankrupt some while ago.

    I have no occupation, but I need money to live. So that is why I sell carpets.

    Viet has no time (nor money) for school. So in his free time, he teaches himself English. He has English books, with the Vietnamese translations included in the books. “I have no occupation, but I need money to live. So that is why I sell carpets”.

    Own experience

    I had learned several things from this job. For instance, how to approach Vietnamese customers and how to be considerate towards the customers. They were all driving by with their scooters and some didn’t want to step off their scooters, so I would have to get the carpets to them and show it while they’re standing.

    Selling the carpets went smoothly, because when the customers came, there was a big chance they would buy a carpet. The hardest part was when there were no customers. We would have to stay at the same place and just wait, which could make you lazy on a mental and physical level. For me these moments were frustrating, because I wanted to do something but I couldn’t.

    Viet was very helpful and friendly. He would help anyone, even when they are just passing by. Furthermore, I liked that he always had a smile on his face, which showed his positive attitude.

    Gift for Viet

    While we were waiting for customers, I noticed that Viet was reading a book and making notes.

    The book was in Vietnamese and English. He was trying to communicate in English as well. This was why I decided to buy a book to help him learn English.

    Viet also told me he dreamed of having a farm of his own. That was why I bought a book on farming.

    Unfortunately, I wasn’t able to reach Viet for the donation. In addition, he was sceptic about the programme. This had let to the distance between us.

    Day 3

    Food prep worker

    The preparation of banh cuon

    Today, I was meeting up with a friend at HuTech University. My friend, Mr. Boy, had been helping with the search of the jobs since day 2.

    During my wait, I was looking around and stopped at a stall, where banh cuon was sold. Banh cuon is a Vietnamese dish and is often a dough sheet filled with a (meat) filling. The first thing the seller asked me, was “Do you want to learn how to cook?”.

    I was really surprised to hear this and I told him about my project. He agreed to let me work with him and tell his story. The seller’s name was Chu Kim.

    After Chu Kim showed his stall, Mr. Boy arrived. We had Chu Kim’s banh cuon as lunch, which tasted great. Afterwards, Chu Kim showed me his house, where he does his preparation.

    The house was nearby and the kitchen was moved to the attic. Chu Kim has done this in order to keep his baby away from the kitchen. When the tour was done, I had to cut the onions with a machine, let the onions dry in the sun, cut the herbs, separate the dough sheets and roll the banh cuon.

    About chu Kim

    As a kid, chu Kim dreamt of earning a lot of money and getting a job as the CEO of a company. “I was young, it was the same as everybody else’s dream. But it didn’t work out, so the dream remains a dream”. Now, at the age of 48, chu Kim is a banh cuon seller.

    Chu Kim has had two accidents, where he broke both his legs and his back. These accidents have constrained him in his work opportunities. He has chosen to be a banh cuon seller, because according to him, this job is not heavy (for his back) and he likes to cook.

    But Chu Kim hasn’t always been the seller of the national dish. In fact, he used to be a barber. By that time, he had already had his first accident, where he had broken his two legs. It took him a lot of time to revalidate. He told me he gained strength from his religion and he did everything the doctor said. The process to walk again went slowly. He had to learn how to move the muscles, the joints and so forth and he had to start with crawling and build it up to walking. But eventually, he was able to walk again. Then he decided to become a barber, because it wasn’t hard on his body/health and it was easy to start a barbershop. Back then, you didn’t need a license or certificate to open a barbershop. Chu Kim told me he bought a simple chair and added a few things to turn it into a hairdresser chair.

    When he was a barber, he visited foster homes to give the children a haircut or he would wash and cut the hairs of the disabled. At the foster homes, he would receive the schedules of the children, so he could plan when to cut the hair of the children. “I really liked it, I felt fortunate” is how he describes his experience with the foster children. But unfortunately, one day he had another accident and broke his back. This had forced him to quit his job as a barber.

    Now, chu Kim is focused on his health and his family. He needs to be healthy, so he can work hard and earn money to buy a house. He wants to buy a house with a lot of land, where he can plant crops and keep/raise animals. He is determined to make this happen within five years, because he wants to give his child the possibility to grow up in a decent house.

    In addition, Chu Kim is putting a lot of effort in his banh cuon, because he believes that if the food is good, customers will come back. He doesn’t mind spending more money and time to specialise in creating his recipe, because the quality and taste are the important parts of the dish.

    Own experience

    It was fun to see how Chu Kim was curious about everything and how he listened to young people, despite the age difference. He was very open and always ready to answer my questions or give his vision on things. And he was patient as well. Despite his health problems, he was still very ambitious. He wanted to make the best banh cuon, so he puts a lot of effort in his preparation.

    Chu Kim was also the first to approach me and ask if I wanted to help him. This showed me how open and welcoming he was.

    Gift for Chu Kim

    Chu Kim wanted to distinct his dish from other banh cuon dishes in terms of taste and quality. The better his banh cuon, the better the sale. So I had bought two cookbooks on cuon (which means “rolled”) dishes and egg rolls. These books can be used as inspiration to perfect his recipe.

    During the day, I had noticed that Chu Kim has a lot of backpain, so I had bought special band aids to ease the pain.

    Day 4

    Crabs seller

    Pay as little

    I decided to go to HuTech University again and look in the surroundings for a new job. Nearby the university is a busy road and there is where I met Dan.

    Dan is a crab seller and he worked with one other guy. At this job, it was important that you are good at bargaining. The seller wants to make profit, while the customer wants to pay as little as possible.

    For me it was difficult to do the bargaining, so they let me weigh the crabs for the customers. Because the prices were not specified, everyone was trying to get the lowest price. It was very different from the other jobs

    About Dan

    Originally from Hai Phong, Dan came to Ho Chi Minh City for three months to observe restaurants and learn how they have achieved their success.

    He is planning to start his own restaurant in his hometown, but first hopes to find the key to success in Ho Chi Minh City.

    I know which way I am going, I know what I want to do, but I need to find out how to succeed

    Dan is very eager to learn. For example, he wants to learn all about selling and understanding the consumer’s behaviour. This was the reason why he has taken the job as a crab seller, while he is in Ho Chi Minh City. Furthermore, he wants to learn how to talk to customers and what customers need. Dan is planning to work in restaurants to gain this knowledge and he hopes the bosses will point out if he has potential for this sector.

    If your family is happy and healthy, that is the most fortunate thing

    Even though Dan is focused on making his dream a reality, his family always comes first. He does everything he can to make his family, which includes his parents, wife and children, happy. “If your family is happy and healthy, that is the most fortunate thing”.

    Own experience

    Dan was very inspiring and in a way, he reminded me of myself. Just like me, he went to a different place for his mission. During the day we didn’t sell much, but from a certain time of the day people kept coming.

    Dan was very open and not afraid to lose or fail. He wasn’t egoistic either and even paid for my lunch. Despite the northern accent, he took the time to express himself so I could understand him.

    Gift for Dan

    I had bought two books for Dan as an aid for his plan to open his own restaurant. One book was about the kitchen from A to Z, which includes hygiene, supplies, preparations among others. The other book was a cookbook with recipes from Ho Chi Minh from an award-winning chef.

    Unfortunately, I wasn’t able to give the donation to Dan, because he couldn’t be reached on his telephone. I tried to find him at the place where we sold the crabs, but he wasn’t there anymore.

    Day 5

    Tourfood seller

    The food storage

    I went to HuTech University again, because I thought I had a bigger chance of finding a job here. At first I had some difficulties with finding a volunteer job, but eventually, after some perseverance, one person let me work with her.

    This person was Co Kim Noi. Co Kim Noi sells bread and little snacks to people who are going on tour. We went to a bus station for touring busses and she let me see the storage of her bread. After some observation, I was also selling bread and snacks.

    About Co Kim Noi

    Co Kim Noi has chosen for this job, so she could be her own boss. She can’t help or take care of others, because of her age and health. She has worked for others before, but there was a lot of pressure and she didn’t have any freedom. With this job, she can come and go as she please.

    As she is getting older, she wants to guide young people to live a good life. She guides her younger family members by giving advice and suggestions.

    You may fall when you first make your way in the world, but you have to overcome all the difficulties by yourself. Stand up and put everything aside. Learn from the difficulties and try to avoid them in the future.

    When I asked her what makes her the happiest, she told me it was health. But she didn’t want to be healthy for herself, she wants to be healthy so she could help her family and friends.

    At the end of the interview, co Kim Noi gave me advice for a good future: “Keep on learning. You can gain success by your own effort.”

    Own experience

    Co Kim Noi was very positive and had a good attitude. Her work was really hard, because she had to stay outside the bus and ask people if they wanted something to eat when they were entering the bus. There was no personal contact but she kept being positive. I admired this very much.

    Everyone was very curious about me and she liked to tell my story. Co Kim Noi and her colleagues treated me well and I was happy to see co Kim Noi liked that I helped her.

    Gift for Co Kim Noi

    Co Kim Noi told me she was the happiest when she is healthy, so she could perform her work. This was why I had decided to buy a set of ginseng-honey bottles, which is very good for the health. In addition, I had given her money, so she could spend it on what makes her happy.

    Day 6

    Streetfood seller

    The heavy baskets

    Today, I met with Mr. Boy. We drove around in the city for a few hours, but eventually I went by foot alone. After a long search, I came across an old lady, selling fruit and snacks. She was already 70 years old, but still working hard.

    I was glad she let me help her with preparing the food by putting the snacks in little bags. When a customer bought fruit, I had to cut the fruit and put it in a bag for the customer.

    About Linh

    There is not much known about Linh. I tried to conduct the interview, but it was very difficult. Sometimes she didn’t understand the question and other times she didn’t how to answer them.

    The lady has been living on the countryside for almost her whole life. She moved to the city just a few years ago. She came to Saigon, because her old job as a farmer was hard. The crops were affected by the weather and location, which resulted in low production and low sales. Her two children are still living on the countryside. She hopes to return home within these five years.

    Own experience

    It was difficult to communicate, because she was illiterate. I showed her the interview, but she couldn’t read it. So we had difficulties understanding each other. She lives day by day, not knowing what she is living for. It was noticeable that she didn’t sell much and was earning just a little. She also declined when I wanted to buy food for her, because she thought it was a waste of money. In the end, I still bought food for her, which she was grateful for. When we said our goodbyes, I could see the sadness in her eyes.

    It was a special moment to see how much she appreciated the time we had spent together.

    Gift for Linh

    The baskets, that the old lady had to carry could be 20 kilo in total. It was hard to imagine she has to walk long distances, while carrying the baskets. She also had scars on her shoulders. This was why I decided to buy medicine for her. In addition, I gave her money, so she can decide how to spend it.

    Day 7

    Waiter

    Serving the food

    I had called an Uber bike, so I could be driven around to search for a job. The driver had a friendly appearance, which encouraged me to ask him if he knew anyone I could help. The driver brought me to his brother-in-law.

    The brother-in-law, who I called Chu Hung, had an accident 22 years ago. The accident was the cause of the paralysation of his lower body. His wife, Co Lien, had taken care of him ever since.

    I told Chu Hung what I was doing in Vietnam and Chu Hung took the time to listen and understand my story. He gave permission to let me work with his wife and told her about my project.

    Afterwards, I helped Co Lien with her work. She sold different dishes, served with rice and rice noodles. Her daughter helped her with serving the food. I got to serve the food too, clean the tables, prepare the cutlery and do the dishes.

    About Co Lien

    Co Lien is a very strong and caring woman. She works hard to support her family and has taken care of her husband and his mother for over 20 years.

    Since young, she has dreamt of being a food seller. She still sees this job as her dream job and has been in this business for 30 years. She started when she was 25. At that time, she was already married to Chu Hung.

    If you have the health, then you have to do anything you can for the society

    Co Lien praises herself lucky, for being able to do her dream job and live with her husband and child. She told me “If you have the health, then you have to do anything you can for the society”. These words sum up the selfless and caring nature of co Lien.

    Own experience

    It was inspiring to see how much Co Lien trusted her husband’s judgement. He suggested her to let me work with her for one day and she agreed with it, without knowing me or my story.

    It was nice to see how well Co Lien and her daughter worked together. Despite being busy, Co Lien kept her calm and could remember every order. It was also funny to see how the customers didn’t notice anything about me, until they heard my accent.

    I have a lot of admiration for Chu Hung and Co Lien. Chu Hung has a positive mindset, despite his condition. Furthermore, Co Lien has taken care of her husband and his mother for 22 years. This takes a lot of love and dedication.

    Gift for Co Lien & Chu Hung

    I wanted to buy (advertising) stickers for the food stand of Co Lien. I asked Chu Hung what he thought of this idea, but he said it wasn’t necessary. So eventually I gave them money, so they could decide how to spend it.
